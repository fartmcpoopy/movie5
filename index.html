Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest

Gore Verbinski
Released in 2006, Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest was directed by Gore Verbinski, the same director as Pirates 1 and 3. It was written by Ted Elliott and Terry Rossio, and it was produced by Jerry Bruckheimer.
Johnny Depp
Playing Captain Jack Sparrow, Johnny Depp is the leading male role in the Pirates franchise. His character remains a fan-favorite to this day, often being referenced in many pop culture instances.
Kiera Knightley
In the Pirates of the Caribbean franchise, Kiera Knightley portrays Elizabeth Swann-- a royal governor's daughter from Port Royale. She is the leading female protagonist in the series. Though obnoxious, her character is important in setting the path of the other characters.
Orlando Bloom
Orlando Bloom plays the other significant male role in the series-- William Turner. Arguably a more prominent character than Jack Sparrow, the story of the Pirates films follows Turner's pursuit towards a relationship with Elizabeth Swann.



Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest follows the events of the first Pirates movie: Curse of the Black Pearl.
The movie begins on Jack's ship, the Black Pearl. Jack realizes he has "the black spot," which means Davy Jones' debt is due. He retreats to an island, grounding his ship there. The scene then switches to the lives of William and Elizabeth, who were both imprisoned by Cutler Beckett. In exchange for his freedom, Beckett proposes an offer to William: retrieve Jack's compass and you will go free. Will accepts Beckett's offer and leaves to find Jack. The story continues as Will and Jack reunite, and Jack forms an idea. He uses William to repay his debt to Jones by forcing him over to the Flying Dutchman. This leads to William finding his father, a servant to the Dutchman. The story progresses as Jack and Will fight against both Davy Jones and Cutler Beckett. In the end, Jack is unable to get past his debt, being sent to the locker by the Kraken. This sets up the plot of the third movie.
The movie is rated PG-13.
